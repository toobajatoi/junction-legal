1. Who this policy applies to

This policy applies to:

  • Users who create an account (email, Google, or Apple Sign-In)
  • Users who play as a guest without signing in
  • Users who purchase subscriptions or in-app products
  • Users who participate in Online Blitz or other multiplayer features
  • Visitors who contact us for support

This policy does not apply to third-party websites, apps, or services that we link to (for example, YouTube videos in Learn lessons, Figma templates, or app store billing pages). Those services have their own privacy policies.

2. Information we collect

We collect information in the categories below. Some information is required to provide core features (for example, account credentials). Other information is optional or collected only when you use specific features.

2.1 Account and identity information

When you register or sign in, we may collect:

  • Email address — if you sign up with email/password or if your OAuth provider shares it
  • Authentication identifiers — Supabase user ID, OAuth subject IDs from Google or Apple
  • Display name — the username you choose during onboarding (must be at least 2 characters)
  • Profile metadata from OAuth — such as your name or profile picture URL if provided by Google or Apple (we primarily use the display name you set in Junction)
  • Password — if you use email sign-up, your password is handled by Supabase Auth; we do not store plain-text passwords on our servers

We do not require a real name. Your display name is shown to other players in Online Blitz matchmaking and results.

2.2 Onboarding and preference information

During first-time setup, you may provide:

  • Age range — selected from predefined brackets (used to tune difficulty; stored in local/cloud progress)
  • Experience level — for example Beginner, Intermediate, or Advanced
  • Interest tags — which game worlds you want to explore (e.g. ecommerce, fintech)

This information is stored as part of your game progress profile.

2.3 Gameplay and progress data

We store gameplay data so you can resume where you left off and, if signed in, sync across devices. This includes:

  • Campaign progress — levels attempted, completed, best scores, stars, and unlock status
  • World and level identifiers — which puzzles you have played
  • Simulation results — scores across metrics such as simplicity, efficiency, conversion, recovery, trust, and accessibility
  • Daily challenge — date, completion status, and best score
  • Blitz mode stats — plays, clears, best score, best time, streaks, and coin balance
  • Vs Computer stats — wins, losses, and draws
  • Learn module progress — lesson quiz attempts, pass/fail status, best scores, and timestamps
  • XP, streaks, and virtual coins — in-game progression and rewards
  • Coin-unlocked levels — campaign levels unlocked early using in-game coins
  • Onboarding completion flag — whether you finished the in-app tutorial
  • Custom node labels — text you enter when adding custom nodes to a puzzle board (stored only as part of your local puzzle state unless shared via UX Audit export)

Most gameplay data for signed-in users is stored in Supabase (user_progress table as JSON). Guest progress is stored locally on your device until you sign in and link your guest session.

2.4 Online Blitz and multiplayer data

If you use Online Blitz, we collect and process:

  • Matchmaking queue entry — your user ID and rating while waiting for an opponent
  • Match records — match ID, level ID, your score, completion time, opponent user ID, opponent score, opponent time, and match outcome
  • Online rating, wins, and losses — stored on your profile for skill-based matchmaking
  • Live board activity — during an active match, we temporarily sync your puzzle nodes, edges, build status, and connection count so your opponent can see your board update in real time. This data is tied to the match and your user ID.
  • Opponent display name and rating — shown to you during and after a match (we do not expose your email to opponents)

We may use bot opponents as a fallback when human matchmaking is unavailable. Bot matches still record scores and outcomes on your profile.

2.5 Purchase and entitlement data

When you buy a subscription or one-time in-app product:

  • Apple App Store or Google Play processes payment. We do not receive your full card number, billing address, or bank details.
  • RevenueCat receives purchase receipts and subscription status from the app store and tells Junction which entitlements you own (for example UX Flow Pro, world unlocks, Learn pack, UX Audit).
  • We store entitlement flags locally and may sync purchase-derived unlock state with your account progress.

We may receive: product identifier, purchase date, renewal status, expiration date (for subscriptions), and anonymous app store transaction IDs through RevenueCat.

2.6 Device, app, and technical data

Depending on your platform and settings, we may collect:

  • Guest device identifier — a random ID stored locally (AsyncStorage) to associate guest display-name claims and guest progress before you sign in
  • Device type and OS version — indirectly through Expo/React Native for compatibility
  • Push notification permission status — if you grant permission, we schedule local reminders on your device (daily streak nudge, weekly challenge). We do not operate a server-side push notification marketing platform at this time.
  • App logs and error reports — our backend providers (Supabase, Expo) may log IP addresses, request timestamps, and error diagnostics for security and reliability. We do not currently integrate a separate third-party crash analytics SDK (such as Sentry or Firebase Analytics) in the app.

We do not collect precise GPS location. We do not access your contacts, photos, microphone, or camera for core gameplay.

2.7 Support and communications

If you email us at toobajatoi44@gmail.com, we receive:

  • Your email address
  • The content of your message and any attachments you choose to send
  • Metadata necessary to respond (timestamps, thread history)

We use this information only to provide support and improve the Service.

2.8 UX Flow Audit and exports

The UX Flow Audit feature generates a structured report from your puzzle solution (metrics, findings, suggested nodes). If you choose Share PDF, the report is rendered on-device and shared through your device's native share sheet (email, messaging apps, cloud storage, etc.). We do not upload audit PDFs to our servers unless you separately send them to us for support.
